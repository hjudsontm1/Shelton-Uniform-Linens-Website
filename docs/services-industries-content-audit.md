# Services + Industries Content Audit

Date: 2026-07-09
Scope: `services.html` and `industries.html` as they exist before any combined-page launch work.

This audit records the current public copy rather than silently replacing it. Shared navigation and footer copy are recorded once because they are identical on both pages. Image assets are noted with their associated content records.

## Status legend

- **Keep**: useful source content that can remain substantially intact.
- **Merge**: useful content that should be normalized into one canonical entry.
- **Rewrite later**: concept is useful, but wording or structure should change after user approval.
- **Retire later**: preserve in this audit, but do not carry into the future canonical page as a standalone item.
- **Needs user approval**: claim, scope, or wording should not be published in the future combined page without approval.

## Cross-page findings

### Duplicate content

1. Goods lists repeat between service cards and industry cards: linens, towels, chef coats, aprons, uniforms, tablecloths, napkins, and wholesale goods.
2. Route language repeats in the Services hero, the event service, the standalone Commercial Route Support card, almost every Industries frequency kicker, both page footers, and quote-oriented CTAs.
3. Finishing language repeats across service bullets and industry problem lists: pressed presentation, folding, packaging, organized return, and wrinkle control.
4. Wholesale support appears as both **Wholesale Laundry & Pressing** and **Dry Cleaner Wholesale** with overlapping production, pressing, finishing, and reliability language.
5. The account-model panel repeats customer-owned goods, rental, and hybrid concepts already used elsewhere on the site.
6. The full footer closing statement, directory, service links, and legal line are duplicated verbatim across both pages.

### Conflicts or approval risks

1. The Industries page publishes exact frequency ranges such as **3-5 pickups weekly** and **1-5 pickups weekly**. Newer approved homepage wording says cadence is built around each account. These ranges may be operationally inaccurate and require user approval.
2. The STR card says **2-5 pickups weekly or route plan by portfolio** but does not state that Shelton's intended model is bulk pickup and return through a central location. It could be misread as house-to-house service.
3. **Event Linen & Restaurant Goods** combines two customer types that the approved homepage explicitly separates.
4. **Commercial Route Support** is presented as a primary service. The approved architecture treats pickup, route cadence, packaging, and return as shared capabilities.
5. **Backup inventory** in the STR list is ambiguous. It could imply Shelton owns, supplies, or stores inventory; the intended responsibility needs confirmation.
6. **Commercial routes across San Diego County** is a geographic coverage claim and should be confirmed before being elevated on a future canonical page.
7. **Reliable replacement cycles** under Restaurants is unclear: it may imply a rental/replacement commitment that has not been defined.
8. **Confidential partner support** and **Quiet partner support** are reasonable concepts but should be approved before becoming prominent claims.

### Outdated or inconsistent wording

- **Dry Cleaner Wholesale** should eventually align with **Wholesale Dry Cleaners / Laundry Partners**.
- **Rental Support** should eventually align with the preferred account-model label **Rental Program** or **Rental**.
- **table linen**, **table linens**, **food-and-beverage linen**, and **event linens** are used inconsistently.
- No use of **textile** appears on either audited page.
- No use of **dry-cleaning** with a hyphen appears on either audited page.

### Missing content

1. **Specialty Cleaning & Restoration** is absent as a service group on both pages.
2. Color retention / color locking, specialty mold removal, chef-coat longevity, and customer-owned-goods protection are not organized as reusable capabilities.
3. Event companies and restaurants are separate on Industries but combined on Services.
4. Casinos & Entertainment appears on Industries but has no dedicated service relationship beyond the Uniform card kicker.
5. The approved homepage **Specialty Commercial Accounts** concept does not appear on either live page. The prescribed future industry list also does not include it, so its final location needs a decision.
6. STR central-location bulk routing is missing from the Industries page.
7. Return formats such as linen carts, poly, bags, and account-specific labels are not normalized and cross-linked.

### Copy requiring explicit approval before publication

- All exact pickup-frequency ranges on `industries.html`.
- Exact San Diego County route-coverage wording.
- Any **500+ cycles** chef-coat longevity claim.
- Any claim that Shelton is the only mold-removal provider in San Diego County.
- Any guaranteed savings, lifespan, replacement-rate, or stain-removal outcome.
- Any language implying Shelton stores event-company inventory.
- Any language implying house-to-house STR pickup.

## Recommended canonical disposition

- Normalize six service groups, nine industry groups, nine shared capabilities, and three account models in one data source.
- Retain current source references and approval states in data so future UI work cannot accidentally publish a flagged claim.
- Treat route and return as a shared capability.
- Keep event companies and restaurants distinct.
- Use approved homepage STR language for central-location bulk pickup.
- Leave both live pages, their navigation entries, and all redirects unchanged until the future combined page is approved.
