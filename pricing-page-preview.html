How pricing works

Commercial laundry pricing is shaped by the work behind the clean goods.

There is no universal flat rate because different operations place different demands on sorting, soil treatment, finishing, packaging, inventory, and routes.

Goods

Market position

The right comparison is total program value, not the lowest cost per pound.

Shelton is not a laundromat or consumer wash-and-fold option. The program sits in the commercial lane: route-based, quality-focused, professionally finished, and built to compete with larger providers where the service model fits.

Quality economics

Better cleaning changes more than the invoice.

Quality can affect appearance, usable life, replacement pressure, staff handling, rejected goods, and customer experience. This is why a commercial program should be evaluated beyond a basic wash price.

Inventory model

Customer-Owned Goods, Hybrid, and Rental are service structures, not pricing tiers.

The right model depends on what the customer already owns, what Shelton may need to supply, and how predictable the recurring inventory need is. The estimator can recommend a direction after it understands the operation.
