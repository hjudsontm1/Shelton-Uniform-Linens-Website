01 — Operation & goods

The same poundage can require very different work.

A hotel sheet, a kitchen apron, and an event tablecloth may weigh the same. The labor inside that pound is different: sorting, soil treatment, finishing, and presentation all change.

Why it matters

Your operation establishes the service pattern. The goods reveal the care within it.

03 — Finish & return

Clean is only part of the service.

Pressing, hanging, bundling, labeling, and protective packaging add labor and determine how easily your team can put finished goods back into use.

Why it matters

The right return format protects presentation and can save your staff meaningful handling time.

04 — Inventory ownership

Who owns the goods changes the service model.

Customer-owned programs center on processing and care. Rental programs also account for supplied inventory, replacements, and the working stock needed between service days.
